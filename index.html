About Anuradhapura

The sacred ancient capital that shaped the soul of Sri Lanka for millennia.

Ancient Kingdom of Glory

Founded in the 4th century BCE, Anuradhapura was the first capital of Sri Lanka and the center of Sinhalese Buddhist civilization for over 1,300 years. A city of grand palaces, monasteries, and magnificent stupas.

When the Bodhi Tree sapling arrived in the 3rd century BCE, this city became the spiritual heart of the island. The Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi still stands today — the oldest tree planted by human hands.

Popular Places

Explore the most sacred and magnificent sites of the ancient kingdom.

Sacred

Sri Maha Bodhi

The oldest documented tree on earth, a direct descendant of the Bodhi Tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment. Planted over 2,300 years ago.

Learn More
Iconic

Ruwanwelisaya

Built by King Dutugemunu in 140 BC, this magnificent white stupa rises 103 meters high. Guarded by 344 elephant statues, it enshrines sacred relics.

Learn More
Historic

Jetavanaramaya

Once the tallest brick structure in the ancient world at 122 meters. Built in the 3rd century CE by King Mahasena, dominating the skyline for centuries.

Learn More
Ancient

Isurumuniya Temple

Built into rock in the 3rd century BC, featuring the famous Isurumuniya Lovers carving and the oldest visible pagoda in the world.

Learn More
Monastic

Abhayagiri Stupa

The centerpiece of a 5,000-monk monastery, this dagoba originally stood 100 meters high. A major center of Buddhist learning attracting scholars from across Asia.

Learn More
Pilgrimage

Mihintale

The cradle of Buddhism in Sri Lanka. Where Arahath Mahinda Thero preached to King Devanampiyatissa in 236 BC. Climb 1,840 steps for panoramic views.
